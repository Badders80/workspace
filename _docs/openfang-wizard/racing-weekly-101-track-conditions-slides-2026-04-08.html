Track Conditions

What Does a Good 4 Really Mean?

A simple guide to NZ track ratings and why the ground matters on race day.

Simple guide for new racing fans
01
Track Conditions
The Scale

NZ uses a 10-point scale.

Firm

1-2

Firmer and faster ground.

Good

3-4

Usually considered ideal racing ground.

Soft

5-7

More give in the surface.

Heavy

8-10

Wetter, more testing conditions.

Clear infographic slide
02
How To Read It
Quick Decode

Small number. Big clue.

Lower number = firmer, faster track
Higher number = softer, wetter track
Should read in under two seconds
03
Why It Matters
Race Day Impact

Track conditions can shape the whole race.

Times

Firmer tracks usually lead to faster times.

Tactics

Jockeys and trainers may change plans depending on the surface.

Performance

Some horses improve sharply when the ground gets softer.

Edge

The right conditions can give one runner a clear advantage.

Keep this practical and easy to scan
04
Horse Types
Not The Same Ground

Not every horse likes the same surface.

Firm Footing

Some horses travel more smoothly when the ground is firmer and faster.

Soft Ground

Others improve when the track gets wetter and more testing.

That is why trainers, owners, and punters all keep an eye on the rating before race day.

More editorial than infographic
05
Quick Tip
Old Terms

Old term Dead 4 is now Good 4.

If you see older racing form using words like Fast, Dead, or Slow, that is the old language.
