## Caption
What does `Good 4` actually mean?

In NZ racing, track conditions are rated on a 10-point scale:
`Firm 1-2`, `Good 3-4`, `Soft 5-7`, `Heavy 8-10`.

Lower number = firmer, faster ground.  
Higher number = softer, wetter ground.

That matters because some horses are much better on certain surfaces, and conditions can change how a race unfolds.

Small number, big clue.

### Color Direction
- Background: primarily white

- Accent: Evolution gold `#d4a964`
- Use gold sparingly for rules, highlights, and emphasis rather than big fills

### Image / Graphic Direction
- Avoid busy photos for every slide
- Prefer one or two clean icons or simple motifs:
  - sun for firm
  - hoof or track line for good
  - cloud for soft
  - rain for heavy
- If a horse image is used, keep it subtle and secondary to the typography
