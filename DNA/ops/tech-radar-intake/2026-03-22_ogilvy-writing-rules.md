## Overview

This note is less about a tool and more about a durable writing pattern worth
keeping close. The old Ogilvy rules landed because they cut straight through
the usual AI-writing noise and reinforce the same direction as newer prose
quality work: clarity, brevity, and writing the way people actually talk.

It does not justify a new stack entry on its own, but it does feel useful as a
companion reference for any anti-slop or prompt-writing work. In that sense it
is better treated as a supporting principle than as a separate workflow.

## How It Works

The mechanism is just a compact set of writing rules.

- Favor simple, direct language.
- Keep writing short enough to hold attention.
- Write to drive understanding and action instead of sounding impressive.

## Takeaways

- Timeless writing advice still maps well onto current AI-output problems.
- Useful as a companion reference for prose-quality experiments.
- Best value is in stealing a few sharp rules, not preserving the whole post as
  a workflow.

## Risks / Caveats

- This is educational guidance, not an executable system.
- Social-post formatting can flatten nuance from the original memo context.
- Easy to collect writing aphorisms without actually changing writing habits.

## If I Revisit This

1. Pull the two or three strongest rules into the writing-quality workflow.
2. Compare them against any stop-slop guidance to avoid overlap.
3. Ignore the rest if they do not change real output quality.
